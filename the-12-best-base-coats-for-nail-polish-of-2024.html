The 12 Best Base Coats For Nail Polish of 2024

When it comes to dynamic duos, the beauty world is full of them: There’s primer and foundation, falsies and lash glue, lip liner and lipstick, and plenty more where that came from. However, there’s one important twosome that’s oftentimes disregarded — base coats and nail polish. While, yes, you can use one without the other, there’s a reason this pairing exists. “A base coat acts as a solid foundation for any nail polish application,” explains nail technician and cosmetologist Alisha Mathis. Aside from just prepping the nail bed for polish, base coats can also strengthen nails, ensure polish doesn't chip, and even provide much-needed nutrients.

So, how do you find the right base coat for your nails? We got you covered. We researched the best options on the market, taking into account what ingredients they’re made of, how long they keep nail polish from chipping, and how well they work when it comes to protecting natural nails. Ultimately, we found 12 knockout base coat formulas that make polish last longer and keep brittle nails from peeling.

Whether you want to nail your at-home manicure or are looking for the perfect pair for your polish, these base coats will provide a sturdy foundation for any lacquer. Ahead, find the best base coats for nail polish of 2024.

What to Keep in Mind

Ingredients

When buying any beauty product, it’s important to consider what ingredients it’s made of. “Generally speaking, try to avoid cosmetic products that contain harsh and toxic chemicals,” says Mathis. “Over the years as a professional in the beauty industry, I have found myself becoming more intentional about awareness of ingredients that come in contact with hair, skin, and nails, and I encourage others to do the same.” In terms of base coats, this means staying away from bad-for-your-nail ingredients like parabens, formaldehyde, formaldehyde resin, toluene, and xylene, among others. Try to also stick to non-toxic nail polish removers and polishes.

As for what you should look for, opt for products with nail-care ingredients like soy, vitamin E, calcium, and zinc. (The Sally Hansen Hard as Nails Nail Hardener, Londontown Grip Lock Base Coat, and Nails Inc. NailPure Base Coat each include some of these ingredients). These ingredients are safe for nails and will help them grow longer, thicker, and stronger over time.

Finish

The finish of your base coat may not be one of your main priorities — it is a base coat after all — but many formulas can be worn on their own to enhance your natural nail. And, just like regular lacquer, base coats come in a few different finishes, including glossy and matte.

The finish really only matters if you don’t plan on layering another polish over top, but if you plan on wearing your base coat on its own, Hermès Les Mains Enamel Base Coat features a unique, translucent white glossy finish that looks gorgeous alone, while OPI Original Nail Envy Nail Strengthener has an almost iridescent finish.

Your Questions, Answered

What is a base coat for nails?

A base coat is a clear or translucent polish that is applied to nails before nail polish. Base coats act as barriers between the nail polish color and the fingernails, which helps defend nails against staining and peeling. They also help keep polish from chipping, and help to harden and strengthen nail beds.

Do you need a base coat for nail polish?

“Base coat is an absolute necessity when polishing nails,” explains Smith. “Base coats help to adhere the polish to the natural nail. You’ll get longer wear out of your color with a base coat than polish alone.” And, contrary to popular belief, base coats aren’t the same as nail primer.

“Primer is used for acrylic nails and gel manicures,” notes Smith. “It helps the acrylic and gel adhere to the nail enhancements by removing oil from the nail plate. It is not necessary for a regular manicure.”

Can you use a top coat as a base coat?

It’s best not to use a top coat as a base coat. “A base coat and top coat are very similar, but a top coat adds shine and has a higher viscosity (meaning it has more of a runny consistency) whereas a base coat adds strength and is typically thicker,” explains Mathis. “Each should be fully applied to the nail plate in the appropriate order for a beautiful, non-chip-prone, polished polish!” If you’re looking for a two-in-one product, though, L.A. COLORS Craze Base Coat is formulated as both a top coat and a base coat.
