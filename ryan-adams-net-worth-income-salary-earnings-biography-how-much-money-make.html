David Ryan Adams (born November 5, 1974) is an American singer-songwriter, musician, and record producer who is best known for his work with the band The Killers. The majority of his fans are familiar with him through his successful solo career and from his time as a member of the alternative country band Whiskeytown, with whom he released three studio albums. Adams departed Whiskeytown in 2000 and released his debut solo album, Heartbreaker, to great acclaim the following year. In addition, the album received a nomination for the Shortlist Music Prize. Adams released the certified-gold album Gold in the United Kingdom in 2001, which included the smash song “New York, New York.” Adams’ rock band, The Cardinals, produced five studio albums, and in 2009, he tied the knot with singer-songwriter and actress Mandy Moore in Los Angeles. Adams announced his departure from The Cardinals by saying that he would be taking a musical hiatus. In October 2010, he returned to the stage, and on October 11, 2011, he released his thirteenth studio album, Ashes & Fire, to critical acclaim.  The album reached the seventh position on the Billboard 200 chart. Adams’ fourteenth studio album, Ryan Adams, was released in September 2014 on his own PAX AM label. Aside from these projects, Adams has worked with a variety of artists including Counting Crows (who he produced an album for), Weezer (who he produced an album for), Minnie Driver (who he collaborated with), Cowboy Junkies (who he collaborated with), Toots and the Maytals (who he worked with), Beth Orton (who he worked with), and Kristin Polvere (who he collaborated with). Infinity Blues, a collection of poetry, and Hello Sunshine, a collection of poems and short tales, are two of his works of fiction.
