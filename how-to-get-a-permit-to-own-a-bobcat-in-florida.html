How To Get A Permit To Own A Bobcat In Florida

People are currently reading this guide.

So You Want a Bobcat in Florida: Turning Your Living Room into a Mini-Pride

Florida: land of sunshine, beaches, and...bobcats as roommates? That's right, folks, in the Sunshine State, owning a bobcat isn't quite as outlandish as wrestling an alligator (though both are highly discouraged for different reasons). But before you dive headfirst into the world of kitty litter pans the size of bathtubs, there's a hurdle to leap: the permit.

Don't Worry, Be Feline Fine: The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC)

The FWC, basically the ultimate deciders of who gets to cuddle a mini-mountain lion, holds the key to your bobcat dreams. Owning a bobcat falls under the category of Class II Wildlife for Personal Use. Now, this isn't a participation trophy kind of permit. The FWC doesn't mess around.

Becoming a Bobcat Bachelor/Bachelorette: Permit Prerequisites with Punch

Here's where things get interesting (and a tad bit complicated). To snag that permit, you'll need to prove you're the ultimate cat whisperer...for wildcats, that is. Buckle up, because you'll need:

  • Experience: We're talking a whopping 1,000 hours of documented experience working with critters like coyotes. So long, Garfield marathons, hello, wilderness internship!
  • Knowledge is Power: You gotta pass a written exam showing you know the A-Z of bobcat biology and proper care. Think "Bobcat 101": diet, habitat needs, the whole whisker lickin' shebang.
  • Accommodation Fit for a Feline: Prepare for a facility inspection. Your bobcat's future digs need to be a luxurious kitty condo, not a sad excuse for a cage. Think spacious enclosures, climbing structures, and maybe even a scratching post the size of a redwood.

The Paper Chase: Don't Get Lost in the Purr-mitology

Once you've got the experience, the knowledge, and the bobcat Taj Mahal ready, it's time to wrangle the paperwork. Head over to the FWC's website and prepare to dive into applications and fees. Remember, this ain't kitten around!

Now, the good news: The permit itself is a reasonable $140, and only needs to be renewed annually.

But wait, there's more! This permit allows you to own a bobcat, but forget about showing it off at the park. For public appearances, you'll need an additional exhibition permit. Just sayin', your neighbors might not appreciate surprise bobcat visits.

Phew! That was a whirlwind, but hey, if you're up for the challenge, the reward is a truly unique companion (just remember, it comes with claws and a taste for small mammals).
