Coles brings back Masterchef knife giveaway promotion for Christmas

The pandemic has meant most of us have spent more time in the kitchen than ever before – which means you’ve definitely noticed if your knives aren’t up to scratch.

Good kitchen knives can be a seriously expensive investment, with some sets costing hundreds of dollars.

But if you’re planning to spend from now until Christmas shopping at Coles then you’ve got the chance to stock up on a chef-approved knife set for free.

The supermarket is once again bringing back its hugely popular MasterChef knives promotion, which sees customers able to earn points in exchange for the kitchen tools.

This year Coles has doubled the range to include a 17. 5cm cleaver, a 9.5cm paring knife, kitchen scissors and a mini chopper.

Coles ambassador and chef Luke Mangan gave the knives the tick of approval, saying they were both “light” and “durable”.

“They’re very sharp, which is a good thing. They’ve got some points to them and for slicing tomatoes they just go through it,” he said.

Mangan also praised the new mini chopper, which can be redeemed for 80 knife credits, saying it was “great for things like mayonnaise, dressings”.

“It’s perfect for little jobs where you don’t want to bring out you food processor or blender,” Coles development chef Michael Weldon added.

“It’s really easy to wash up too and best of all, it’s sharp.”

To score the freebie knives, all shoppers have to do is scan their Flybuys card when they spend more than $20 in a single transaction, earning them one MasterChef knife credit.

The knives cost between 10 and 80 credits each, however, Coles has also offered customers a “half pay” option this year with all the knives.

Instead of using say 20 MasterChef knife credits to pay for the paring knife, shoppers can instead use 10 credits and pay $10 to make up the difference.

There’s also a MasterChef wooden block to house the knives that can be purchased for $35.

Coles launches Christmas range early

It might be only October but Coles has already unveiled its Christmas food collection for the silly season, with over 150 new or improved products hitting shelves starting later this month.

There’s a Smoked Crackling Ham Rib Rack Roast, which Coles promises to have their “best crackling” ever after extensive recipe development this year.

“For me it’s the centre of the Christmas table this year,” Mr Weldon said. “it’s an absolute star.”

Last year’s popular stuff salmon roast is back in a new “jazzed up” version, this time coming with a “thick” layer of herb, butter and lemon stuffing.

For dessert Coles have two “restaurant quality” sweet treats on offer a Mirror Glaze Mousse Raspberry, Vanilla and Belgian White Chocolate log.

“It’s a raspberry glaze on the outside with a white chocolate mousse with a little bit of vanilla through it in the centre and then a nice slab of raspberry jelly on top with a little bit of sponge,” Mr Weldon said.

“It’s designed to be able to cut in and you get nice even slices … you can slice eight or 10 slices.”

More Coverage

For dark chocolate lovers there’s a Mirror Glaze Mousse Belgian Chocolate and Salted Caramel log – which also happens to be vegan.

In a first for the supermarket it will also be selling 100 per cent plastic-free and recyclable Christmas crackers.

The “rustic” style crackers are made from FSC paper and contain a joke, paper hat and puzzles – all the usual trimmings minus the plastic.
