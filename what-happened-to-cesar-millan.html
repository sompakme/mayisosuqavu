What Happened To Cesar Millan?

Cesar Millan, known to many as “The Dog Whisperer,” rose to fame with his uncanny ability to rehabilitate problem dogs and train their owners. His television show, “Dog Whisperer with Cesar Millan,” which aired from 2004 to 2012, made him a household name. However, in the years following the show’s conclusion, Millan faced a series of personal and professional challenges that led many to wonder, “What happened to Cesar Millan?” This article delves into the life of Cesar Millan post-“Dog Whisperer,” exploring his setbacks, triumphs, and current endeavors.

Early Life and Rise to Fame

Before we explore what happened to Cesar Millan, it’s important to understand his background. Millan was born in rural Culiacán, Sinaloa, Mexico, and grew up working with animals on his grandfather’s farm. His natural affinity for dogs earned him the nickname “El Perrero,” meaning “the dog boy.” In 1990, he crossed the border into the United States with big dreams but little money and no knowledge of English.

Establishing the Dog Psychology Center

Millan’s passion for dogs led him to found the Dog Psychology Center in South Los Angeles, where he focused on rehabilitating severely aggressive dogs. His unique approach to dog training caught the attention of the media, and soon after, he became the star of “Dog Whisperer with Cesar Millan.”

The Success of “Dog Whisperer with Cesar Millan”

The show was an instant hit, making Millan an international celebrity. He authored several best-selling books and created a magazine, “Cesar’s Way.” His methods, though sometimes controversial, resonated with many dog owners struggling to manage their pets’ behavioral issues.

Personal and Financial Struggles

Despite his professional success, Millan’s personal life was fraught with difficulties. In 2010, his wife of 16 years filed for divorce. The following year, Millan faced another blow when his beloved pit bull, Daddy, passed away. These events took a toll on his emotional well-being, leading to a suicide attempt in May 2010.

Bankruptcy and Recovery

Financial troubles followed his personal struggles. In 2012, it was reported that Millan was in financial dire straits, with his company, Cesar Millan Inc., filing for bankruptcy. Millan cited the divorce, along with bad business decisions, as the primary reasons for his financial downfall.

Rebuilding His Brand and Career

Despite these setbacks, Millan was determined to rebuild his life and career. He began by focusing on his health and well-being and then worked on revitalizing his brand.

New Television Shows

Millan returned to television with a new series, “Leader of the Pack,” in 2013, which aimed to raise awareness about the global issue of abandoned dogs. He also launched “Cesar 911,” another series where he tackled some of the most challenging dog behavior problems.

Continued Advocacy for Dogs

Millan has remained an advocate for dogs, particularly focusing on the plight of shelter dogs. He has worked to educate the public on the importance of adoption and responsible pet ownership.

Expansion into Digital Platforms

Recognizing the shift in media consumption, Millan has expanded his presence into digital platforms. He has a strong social media following and offers online training courses and advice through his website.

Personal Life Today

On a personal front, Millan found love again and announced his engagement to Jahira Dar in 2016. He credits Dar with helping him rebuild his life after his suicide attempt and financial collapse.

Legal Issues and Controversies

Millan’s methods have not been without criticism. He has faced legal issues, including a lawsuit in 2016 when a dog trained at his center attacked a woman. Critics argue that his techniques are outdated and can be harmful to dogs.

Current Projects and Ventures

Today, Millan continues to work on various projects, including a line of dog products and a new training facility, the Cesar Millan Dog Psychology Center in Santa Clarita, California.

Philanthropic Efforts

Millan’s love for dogs extends to his philanthropic efforts. He established the Cesar Millan Foundation, which provides financial support to animal shelters and promotes spaying and neutering to control the pet population.

Legacy and Impact on Dog Training

Millan’s legacy in the world of dog training is significant. He has changed the way many people view dog behavior and training, emphasizing the role of the owner in a dog’s behavioral development.

Conclusion

Cesar Millan’s journey has been one of dramatic highs and lows. From his humble beginnings to his rise as a television star and subsequent personal and financial challenges, Millan has shown resilience and a continued passion for dogs. While he has faced criticism and controversy, his impact on dog training and rehabilitation is undeniable. Today, he remains a prominent figure in the world of animal behavior, with ongoing projects and a commitment to helping dogs and their owners live harmonious lives.

FAQ Section

  • What is Cesar Millan doing now?
    Cesar Millan continues to work with dogs and their owners through his Dog Psychology Center, produces new television content, and runs a line of dog care products.
  • Did Cesar Millan’s methods receive criticism?
    Yes, some of Millan’s training methods have been criticized by animal behaviorists and veterinarians who claim they can be harmful and promote aggression in dogs.
  • How has Cesar Millan impacted dog training?
    Millan has popularized the concept of “pack leadership” in dog training and has encouraged owners to establish themselves as calm and assertive leaders.

In summary, Cesar Millan’s story is one of resilience and dedication to the well-being of dogs worldwide. Despite facing numerous challenges, he has managed to maintain his status as a respected figure in the realm of dog training and continues to contribute to the field through various endeavors.
