Early Life & Education

Born on September 20, 1976, Ainsley Earhardt, an American journalist and television host, originally hails from Spartanburg, South Carolina. In her early years, her family moved to the Foxcroft neighbourhood in Charlotte, North Carolina, but later relocated to the Columbia region of South Carolina while she was still in elementary school. 

Ainsley‘s father is identified as Lewie Wayne Earhardt, while her mother is Dale Earhardt. Both parents actively participated in raising Ainsley and her two siblings, Elise Giles Earhardt and Trenton Graham Earhardt. The journalist and television host’s journey began in Spartanburg, and her subsequent relocations didn’t hinder her rise in the media world.

Ainsley Earhardt graduated from the University of South Carolina, where she bagged her BA.

Career

Ainsley Earhardt embarked on her career as a reporter with the CBS station WLTX in Columbia, South Carolina, where she eventually became a well-known news anchor. Serving from 2000 to 2004, she anchored morning and lunchtime news segments. 

Notably, after the September 11 attacks, Earhardt travelled to New York City to cover an inspiring story about middle school students from South Carolina who generously donated $500,000 for firefighters. This heartfelt contribution was utilized to replace a lost fire truck at the World Trade Center site by acquiring a new one.

In 2007, Ainsley Earhardt decided to relocate to New York City and join Fox News Channel. Despite lacking a political background, her impressive abilities caught the attention of Roger Ailes, leading to her recruitment by the network. A standout segment, “Ainsley Across America,” was featured on Sean Hannity‘s show. 

Over the years, she has co-hosted various programs, including Fox and Friends Weekend, All-American New Year’s Eve, and America’s News Headquarters. Additionally, Ainsley Earhardt has shared her insights as a panellist on The Live Desk and Greg Gutfeld‘s Red Eye.
