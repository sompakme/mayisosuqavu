West London football legend Fred Callaghan dies aged 77 as tributes pour in

Tributes have been pouring in for former Fulham footballer Fred Callaghan, 77, who died on Tuesday afternoon (September 13). Born in Parsons Green, Callaghan made more than 300 appearances for Fulham over a decade, during which he earned himself the nickname 'The Tank'.

Following his retirement from football in 1974, Callaghan had a period as manager and assistant manager for Brentford FC, also based in West London. He even became a black cab driver in London and in 2012, he became a corporate hospitality host for his former club Fulham.

Football fans have taken to social media to pay their respects, with one fan writing: "Fred was one of my original heroes, so much so that when I played football at school, I started as a left back. I had that classic shirt with the badge and [number] 3 on. Fred was a smashing character and lovely man. Condolences to his family. RIP Fred and thanks for the memories."

Another fan shared his memories of Callaghan, saying: "So sorry to read this, he was always a favourite. I remember at Charlton we were 2-1 down with minutes to go. Fred never came up for corners but when he did this time I knew he'd be the one to get the equaliser. Can see it now."

A statement posted by Callaghan's former club Fulham read: "Everyone at Fulham was saddened to learn of the passing of Club legend Fred Callaghan on Tuesday afternoon.

"Born just down the road in Parsons Green, Fred gave a decade of service to the Whites as a player, making his debut in a 2-2 draw away to Aston Villa in March 1964, the first of 336 appearances he would go on to rack up for the Club. Nicknamed The Tank on account of his storming runs down the wing and dangerous crosses, Fred primarily appeared at left-back or left-half."

He is survived by his wife Hazel, as well as his children.
