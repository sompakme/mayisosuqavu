Early Life and Career Beginnings

Joe Piscopo was born in Passaic, New Jersey, and grew up in North Caldwell. He attended West Essex High School and later graduated from Jones College in Jacksonville, Florida, where he received a degree in broadcast management. Piscopo began his career in the entertainment industry as a stand-up comedian, performing in various clubs in New York City.

Breakthrough with Saturday Night Live

Piscopo’s big break came in 1980 when he was selected as a cast member for the sixth season of Saturday Night Live (SNL). His impressions of celebrities like Frank Sinatra and David Letterman, among others, made him a household name. His tenure at SNL significantly boosted his career and contributed to his net worth.

Post-SNL Career

After leaving SNL in 1984, Piscopo ventured into other areas of entertainment. He starred in several films and TV shows, including “Johnny Dangerously,” “Sidekicks,” and “Law & Order.” He also released a few music albums, further diversifying his income streams.

Radio Career
