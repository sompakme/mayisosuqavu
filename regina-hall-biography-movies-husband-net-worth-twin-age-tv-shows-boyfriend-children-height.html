Biography

Regina Lee Hall (born December 12, 1970) is an accomplished American actress who has been a prominent figure in the entertainment industry for over two decades. Her career, which began in the early 2000s, has been marked by a series of notable performances in both television and film.

She is most famously known for her role as Brenda Meeks in the comedy horror Scary Movie series, a character that showcased her versatility as an actress. She has also appeared in several hit television shows, including Ally McBeal, Law & Order: LA, Grandfathered, and Black Monday, where she has consistently demonstrated her range and depth as a performer.

Regina Hall has starred in several critically acclaimed feature films, including The Best Man and its sequel, The Best Man Holiday, About Last Night, Vacation, Girls Trip, The Hate U Give, and Little. Her ability to bring nuance and depth to her roles has earned her widespread recognition, culminating in her being the first African American actress to win the New York Film Critics Circle Award for Best Actress for her performance in the comedy-drama film Support the Girls.

Regina Hall‘s dynamic range as an actress, her ability to portray complex characters with depth and authenticity, and her continued commitment to excellence has cemented her status as a respected and admired figure in the entertainment industry.

Early Life & Education

Regina Hall, a celebrated actress of American origin, was born on December 12, 1970, in Washington, D.C., USA. Her parents were Odie Hall, a proficient contractor and electrician, and Ruby Hall, an accomplished teacher.

Upon completing her secondary education at Immaculata College High School, she pursued higher education at Fordham University, obtaining a Bachelor’s degree in English and a Master’s degree in Journalism from New York University. Regina Hall thirsts for knowledge, as she once expressed her willingness to remain in school “as long as somebody would’ve paid my rent.”

Career

Regina Hall‘s career has been a journey of surprises and successes, each new chapter building upon the last. It all began in 1997 when the then-26-year-old actress appeared in her first television commercial. This led to a role on the soap opera Loving, followed by guest appearances on the Fox police drama New York Undercover.

But it wasn’t until 1999 that Regina Hall made a name for herself, thanks to her breakout performance in the film The Best Man, where she played the character of Candy Sparks. This role opened up many doors for the rising star, leading to other notable appearances in Love and Basketball, Scary Movie and its sequels, Disappearing Acts, Ally McBeal, Paid in Full, Malibu’s Most Wanted, King’s Ransom, and The Honeymooners.

Between 2009-2014, Regina Hall had some of her most notable roles. In 2009 she appeared in Law Abiding Citizen 2010. She starred in Mardi Gras and played Deputy District Attorney Evelyn Price on Law & Order: LA from 2010 to 2011. In 2012, she starred in Think Like a Man and reprised her role as Candace Sparks in The Best Man Holiday in 2013.

2014 was a big year for Regina Hall. She starred alongside Kevin Hart in About Last Night, which garnered praise from critics for their pairing. The duo expressed interest in working on more projects, and she reflected on her experience in ensemble films.

In 2022, she was honored to co-host the 94th Academy Awards alongside Amy Schumer and Wanda Sykes. She had previously received recognition for her performance in Support the Girls, earning her a nomination for the New York Film Critics Circle Award for Best Actress and making her the first African-American recipient.

Regina Hall has also had memorable roles in films such as Barbershop: The Next Cut, The Hate U Give, and Girls Trip. She has even agreed to do a comedy special for Showtime through her company RH Negative, further showcasing her range as a performer.
