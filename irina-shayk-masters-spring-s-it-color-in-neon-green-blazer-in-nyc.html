Irina Shayk Masters Spring's It Color in Neon-Green Blazer in NYC

Irina Shayk is nailing transition dressing.

The model was captured by the paps again out and about in New York City's moody weather, this time wearing a neon-green blazer look she styled in a relatively low-key manner. Sporting a super-bright oversized jacket from Ukrainian label Gasanova, Shayk kept the rest of her ensemble simple, opting for a second-skin little black slipdress from SKIMS. And to keep the bold hue wearable for the everyday, Shayk reached for a pair of leather combat boots by Tamara Mellon.

It seems that, like us, Bradley Cooper's ex has missed getting dressed every day. She chose the standout jacket for a day spent with the actor and their young daughter, Lea De Seine Shayk Cooper. Complementing her off-duty outfit, she carried By Far's cult mini Rachel bag and rocked early-aughts favorite Chrome Hearts' sold-out mirror sunglasses. As for glam, she went with a no-makeup-makeup look and let her loose waves down to match the overall laid-back feel.

The model is no stranger to incorporating bright colors into her everyday lineup. Last week, while running errands around Manhattan, Shayk channeled the '90s, seemingly inspired by beloved Clueless character Cher Horowitz, with a bold purple-and-yellow plaid skirt suit from iconic designer Vivienne Westwood's spring 2021 collection. Again, adhering to her cool-girl, off-duty vibes, the mom-of-one opted for another pair of her go-to combat bootsâ€”ensuring that the ensemble was as practical as it was chic.
