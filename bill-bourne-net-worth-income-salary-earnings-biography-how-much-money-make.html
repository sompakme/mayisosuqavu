In 1954, Bill Bourne was born William Sigurd Bourne in Red Deer, Alberta. He is a Canadian musician and songwriter who has collaborated with a number of artists, including Alan MacLeod, Shannon Johnson, Lester Quitzau, Madagascar Slim, Aysha Wills, Eivr Paalsontir, Wyckham Porteous, and Jasmine ‘Jas’ Ohlhauser, among others. A musical family from rural Alberta, Bill’s musical education started at the age of two, when he would sleep behind the piano during country dances where his parents’ band would be performing. Bill’s music is varied and rich in traditional music influences from across the globe, and he has been nominated for and won many Juno Awards in Canada. He performs on guitar and sings with a deep and soulful tone; his songs tell tales that are both lyrical and life affirming; he lives in Los Angeles. Throughout his musical compositions, he incorporates dance rhythms from his early youth. He now lives in the Canadian city of Edmonton.
